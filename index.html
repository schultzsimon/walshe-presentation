The New Discipline

"The New Discipline is a term I've adopted to describe compositional work which is aware of the inherent musical qualities and information in all the semiotic channels of the performers' physical and mental presence and their physical and mental interaction with things."
â€” Jennifer Walshe, 2016

In her seminal text, Walshe articulates a framework for understanding composition that extends beyond traditional musical notation to encompass the totality of performance:

  • Integration of physical theater, choreography, and visual elements
  • Recognition of the body as an instrument with its own history and vocabulary
  • Acknowledgment of the performer's entire physical and mental presence
  • Treating composition as a holistic practice that transcends disciplinary boundaries
  • Emphasis on flexibility and adaptability across different media and contexts

The New Discipline represents a significant shift from traditional composition, requiring practitioners to develop a flexible mindset capable of navigating multiple artistic domains simultaneously.
